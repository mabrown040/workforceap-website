Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about our programs, admissions, and job placement

Admissions

What are the eligibility requirements?
To apply to WorkforceAP, you must be: (1) 18 years or older, (2) a U.S. citizen or permanent resident, (3) possess a high school diploma or GED, (4) committed to 100% program completion, (5) willing to participate in job placement services, and (6) have access to a reliable internet connection and computer. We welcome applicants from all backgrounds, including those with no prior tech experience.
Do I need prior tech experience to apply?
No! WorkforceAP programs are designed for beginners with no technical background. We start with fundamentals and build your skills progressively. We've successfully trained career-changers, unemployed workers, and students with zero tech experience. The most important thing is your commitment to learning and completing the program.
What is the application process?
Our application process is straightforward: (1) Complete our online application form with your personal info and program interest, (2) Our admissions team reviews your application within 48 hours, (3) If you're a good fit, we'll schedule a brief intake call to discuss your goals and answer questions, (4) Upon approval, you'll receive your start date and onboarding materials. The entire process typically takes 5-7 business days.
Is there an application fee?
No! There is absolutely no application fee. WorkforceAP is completely free to apply for and free to attend. Our programs are fully funded through partnerships with major corporations and community organizations. You'll never pay tuition, enrollment fees, or hidden costs.
When do programs start?
We run cohorts on a rolling basis throughout the year. New cohorts typically start monthly, though some programs have cohorts starting bi-weekly. Once you're approved, we'll connect you with the next available start date for your selected program. You can also request to wait for a future cohort if timing works better for you.

Programs

How long are the programs?
Most WorkforceAP programs run 8-12 weeks depending on the course. We offer flexible scheduling with both full-time and part-time options to fit your lifestyle. Full-time programs typically require 30-40 hours per week, while part-time options average 15-20 hours per week. You'll have access to course materials for 6 months after graduation to review anytime.
What programs do you offer?
We offer industry-recognized training in: (1) AI Fundamentals & Machine Learning, (2) IT Support Technician (CompTIA A+, Network+), (3) Cybersecurity Essentials, (4) Cloud Computing (AWS/Azure), (5) Data Analytics & Business Intelligence, (6) Web Development & Full Stack, (7) Database Administration (SQL/NoSQL), (8) Network Administration (Cisco), and (9) Project Management & Agile. All programs include hands-on labs, real-world projects, and industry certifications.
What certifications will I earn?
Certifications vary by program but include industry-recognized credentials like Google Cloud Certifications, CompTIA A+ and Network+, Cisco certifications, AWS Solutions Architect Associate, Microsoft Azure Administrator, and Project Management Professional (PMP). All certifications are highly valued by employers and remain valid for 3+ years. You'll receive official digital badges and certificates upon completion.
Are classes online or in-person?
We offer both! Most of our training is available online for flexibility and convenience. However, we also offer in-person cohorts in the Austin area for students who prefer classroom learning or need more hands-on instruction. When you apply, you can indicate your preference, and we'll match you with the format that works best for your situation.
What technology do I need?
You'll need: (1) A computer (Mac, Windows, or Linux - at least 4GB RAM recommended), (2) Reliable internet connection (25+ Mbps recommended for video), (3) Webcam and microphone for live sessions, (4) Ability to install software (admin access helpful). We provide all course materials and cloud-based labs, so you won't need expensive software. Our IT Support Team can help with technical setup during onboarding.

Job Placement

Is job placement guaranteed?
While we can't guarantee employment, we have a 90% job placement rate for graduates who actively participate in our placement services. We provide resume coaching, interview prep, LinkedIn optimization, and direct introductions to 200+ hiring partners. Success depends on your effort - those who complete the program, engage with our placement team, and actively interview typically land roles within 30-60 days.
What is the average salary after graduation?
Average starting salaries for our graduates range from $55,000-$75,000 depending on the program and location. IT Support roles start around $50,000-$60,000, while Cloud/Data roles average $65,000-$85,000. After 2 years of experience, many graduates earn $75,000-$120,000+. These figures are based on actual salary reports from graduates placed in the last 24 months.
How does job placement support work?
Our comprehensive placement support includes: (1) Resume optimization for ATS systems, (2) Interview coaching (behavioral & technical), (3) LinkedIn profile optimization, (4) Professional networking events, (5) Direct introductions to hiring partners, (6) Salary negotiation guidance, (7) Post-placement support (first 90 days). Your dedicated job coach works with you 1-on-1 starting in week 4 of your program.
Who are your employer partners?
We partner with 200+ employers across tech, healthcare, finance, and government sectors. Our partners include Fortune 500 companies, fast-growing startups, and local Austin businesses. Notable partners include Google Cloud, Amazon Web Services, Microsoft, Dell Technologies, IBM, EY, and local companies like HomeAway, RetailMeNot, and Oracle Austin. Many actively recruit from our graduation cohorts.
What if I don't find a job after graduation?
We provide extended support for graduates who don't land roles immediately. Our placement team continues working with you for up to 12 months after graduation at no additional cost. We offer: (1) Continued interview coaching, (2) Portfolio project guidance, (3) Additional certifications (if needed), (4) Networking introductions, (5) Career consulting. Most graduates who engage with these services find roles within 6 months.

Billing & Support

Is the program really 100% free?
Yes! WorkforceAP offers completely free training, certifications, and job placement assistance. There are zero tuition fees, no hidden costs, and no surprise charges. Our programs are fully funded through partnerships with major corporations, government workforce development grants, and community organizations. Your only investment is your time and commitment to learning.
Can I get financial assistance?
The program itself is free! We also have partnerships with workforce development agencies that may provide additional support for eligible participants, including living stipends, childcare assistance, or technology grants. We'll discuss your situation during your intake call and connect you with available resources. Some graduates have received $500-$2,000+ in additional support.
What if I need to take a break?
Life happens! If you need to pause your studies due to illness, family emergencies, or work conflicts, we offer flexible options: (1) Pause your current cohort and join a future one (within 12 months), (2) Adjust to part-time schedule, (3) Work with instructors on extension timeline. We want you to succeed, so we're willing to work with you on solutions that fit your circumstances.
What support services are available?
Beyond academics, we provide: (1) 1-on-1 mentoring and tutoring, (2) Career coaching and resume help, (3) Mental health resources and stress management, (4) Technology support for software/hardware issues, (5) Interview practice with industry professionals, (6) Peer study groups and cohort networking, (7) Alumni network access. Our goal is your complete success, not just completing coursework.

Technical & Support

What if I have technical difficulties during the program?
We have a dedicated IT Support Team available during all live sessions and office hours. If you encounter technical issues, you can: (1) Contact our support team via chat during sessions, (2) Email support@workforceap.org for non-urgent issues, (3) Schedule a 1-on-1 tech support appointment, (4) Access our troubleshooting knowledge base. Most issues are resolved within 24 hours. We also provide pre-course tech setup to prevent common problems.
Do you offer accommodations for accessibility needs?
Absolutely. We're committed to accessibility and offer: (1) Closed captions on all video lectures, (2) Screen reader compatible learning materials, (3) Flexible scheduling for medical appointments, (4) Extended time for exams if needed, (5) One-on-one tutoring for specific challenges, (6) Adjusted workload if necessary. When you apply, please let us know about any accommodations you need, and we'll work with you to ensure success.
Can I download course materials for offline access?
Yes! All course materials, lectures, and labs are downloadable and accessible offline. You can watch videos, review notes, and study anywhere - on the bus, in your car, or at home without internet. Once downloaded, materials remain yours forever. We also provide physical USB drives with all materials for the first week's content, so you can get started even before your cohort officially begins.
